The energy audit indicates significant inefficiencies in HVAC systems across multiple areas, with consumption levels exceeding expected benchmarks by approximately 30%.

Cooling systems are running continuously during non-operational hours, indicating poor scheduling and lack of automation.

Lighting infrastructure relies heavily on outdated fluorescent technology, leading to higher electricity usage compared to LED alternatives.

The building suffers from poor insulation, especially in conference rooms and upper floors, resulting in energy loss and increased cooling demand.

The data center is the largest contributor to energy consumption, accounting for nearly 40% of total usage due to continuous equipment operation and inefficient cooling mechanisms.

There is heavy dependence on grid electricity, which increases the carbon footprint and operational costs.

Opportunities for improvement include:
- Implementing smart thermostats for HVAC optimization
- Retrofitting lighting systems with LED fixtures
- Improving insulation and sealing air leaks
- Installing rooftop solar panels to reduce grid dependency
- Optimizing data center cooling efficiency

Adopting these measures could reduce total energy consumption by 20–30% and significantly lower emissions.
